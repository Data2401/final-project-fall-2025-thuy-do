Introduction

  • College majors greatly shape graduates income, job stability, and career direction.
  • While many assume STEM majors always earn the most, the data reveal a more complex story.
  • This project explores how salaries, unemployment, and major size interact to show real-world outcomes across fields.

1. Dataset

Dataset: FiveThirtyEight College Majors
Source: GitHub

Columns include:

  • Major

  • Major_category

  • Total, Employed, Employed_full_time_year_round

  • Unemployed, Unemployment_rate

  • Median

Why Its Interesting

  • Compare assumptions vs.reality: Are STEM majors always highest-paid?
  • Examine employment outcomes across majors
  • Identify majors with unexpectedly high/low salaries or unemployment rates
  • Highlight career insights for future graduates

Project Focus

We focus on:

  1. Salary trends: Which majors pay well? Any exceptions?

  2. Employment outcomes: High-paying majors with high unemployment?

  3. Major size effect: Does the number of graduates impact outcomes?

  4. Category comparison: STEM vs.non-STEM fields

Research Questions

  • Which majors are unexpectedly high-paying or low-paying compared to their category stereotype?
  • Are there majors with high unemployment despite high median salary, or vice versa?
  • Which majors have the top salaries overall?
  • Do larger graduating classes in a major correlate with better employment outcomes?

Salary by Major Category

majors %>%
group_by(Major_category) %>%
summarise(avg_salary = mean(Median)) %>%
  
ggplot(aes(x = reorder(Major_category, avg_salary), y = avg_salary, fill = avg_salary)) +
geom_col() +
coord_flip() +
labs(title = "Average Median Salary by Major Category",
x = "Major Category",
y = "Average Median Salary (USD)") +
theme_minimal() +
theme(legend.position = "none")

  • STEM majors generally highest-paid

  • Business and Healthcare show strong salaries

  • Humanities and Arts lower on average

Median Salary vs.Unemployment Rate

majors %>%
  ggplot(aes(x=Median, y=Unemployment_rate, size=Total, color=Unemployment_rate)) +
  geom_point(alpha=0.7) +
  scale_size(range=c(2, 12), guide=guide_legend(title="Total Graduates")) +
  scale_color_gradient(low="lightblue", high="darkblue", guide=guide_colorbar(title="Unemployment Rate")) +
  scale_x_continuous(labels=scales::dollar) +
  labs(
    x="Median Salary",
    y="Unemployment Rate",
    title="Median Salary vs. Unemployment Rate, Sized by Total Graduates"
  ) +
  theme_minimal()

Top 10 highest-paying majors

top10 <- majors %>%
arrange(desc(Median)) %>%
slice_head(n=10)

ggplot(top10, aes(x=reorder(Major, Median), y=Median)) +
geom_segment(aes(x=Major, xend=Major, y=0, yend=Median), color="gray") +
geom_point(color="darkred", size=4) +
coord_flip() +
labs(x="Major", y="Median Salary", title="Top 10 Highest-Paying Majors") +
theme_minimal()

  • Engineering and Computer Science dominate

  • Healthcare fields appear in top 10

  • Significant pay gap between top majors
